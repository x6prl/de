müssen
Main idea: müssen can express both internal and external necessity.
Internal necessity: something the speaker feels they need to do.
Example: Ich muss mit dir reden. = I need to talk to you.
External necessity: rules, circumstances, pressure from outside.
Example: Ich muss morgen arbeiten. = I have to work tomorrow.

müssen itself does not always show whether the necessity is internal or external. The context usually tells you.

One of the typical German uses: Musst du immer ...?
Example: Musst du immer fernsehen? Wir können doch etwas spielen.
Translation: Do you always have to watch TV? We could play something.
meaning: not a real question about obligation, but a complaint or annoyance.
Why are you always watching TV?
So here musst du immer often means emotional force, not literal necessity.
