## Important limitation

This does **not** mean that every listed particle can be freely combined with any verb. The list identifies **recognized types of first components** in separable verb formations. In real usage, what matters is the established lexical verb: **ankommen, mitnehmen, hinausgehen, teilnehmen, preisgeben, zurechtkommen**, and so on. ([Grammis][1])

## What is most useful in practice

For learners, the most important high-frequency separable particles are:

**ab-, an-, auf-, aus-, ein-, mit-, nach-, vor-, zu-, weg-, zurück-, zusammen-, her-, hin-, heraus-, hinein-, vorbei-, weiter-, fort-.**

These are the particles that show up very often in everyday verbs such as **aufstehen, ankommen, ausgehen, mitbringen, zurückkommen, hineingehen**. ([Grammis][1])

## Final corrected conclusion

So the corrected answer in English is:

* German does not really have a neat closed list of “all separable prefixes” in the strict sense.
* The more accurate category is **separable verb particles**.
* Official sources provide a fairly broad inventory of such first components.
* For actual learning, it is better to focus on the **common separable particles** and on **individual verbs**, rather than trying to memorize the entire official inventory at once. ([Grammis][1])
